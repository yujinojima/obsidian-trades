> selection must be composite (`candidate_score`).

## Selection rule (new — v2)

A market is a candidate for a paper trade only if:

1. Passes all safety gates (spread ≤ $0.015, liquidity ≥ $10,000, ≥ 24h to close, ≥ $0.02 low-price)
2. Produces the **highest `candidate_score`** among markets that also have at least one
   **firing strategy signal**
3. Is NOT AVOIDed by `news_event_no_trade_filter` (now master-gate — tightened today)

### `candidate_score` components (additive, transparent)

- `liquidity_score` — log-scaled, saturates at $1M
- `spread_score` — 1.0 at zero spread, 0.0 at $0.02 spread
- `time_to_resolution_score` — peak 2–30 days, decays outside
- `low_price_score` — peak in $0.05–$0.35 band; penalty at dust and near-unity
- `ambiguity_penalty` — slug-keyword heuristic (jesus / alien / apocalypse / …)
- `news_risk_penalty` — tight time-to-close OR news-keyword slug OR large 24h move

Source of truth: `polymarket-bot/scripts/polymarket_strategy_scan.py` → `candidate_score()`.

## Scanner bugs found & fixed

1. **Pre-filter ordering** — early run sorted before safety-filtering, putting dust + expired
   markets at the top. Fixed: filter, then rank.
2. **`tick_size` wrong field** — used `orderMinSize` (min notional $5) as price tick,
   producing $5.02 proposed-price. Fixed: hardcoded to Polymarket's $0.01 uniform tick.
3. **Selection by lowest-price-only** — first "best candidate" was Belgium WC at $0.0205
   purely on price; composite ranking never applied. Fixed: `candidate_score()` with 6
   additive components + 2 penalties; ranking is `candidate_score desc`.
4. **News filter too narrow** — original rule AVOIDed only markets < 7 days to close. A
   36-days-out Iranian-regime market slipped through. Fixed: extended to AVOID news-keyword
   slugs across 90-day horizon, plus expanded keyword list (Iran, Israel, election, nuclear,
   BTC price brackets, etc.). Also promoted to **master filter** — any market it AVOIDs
   forces all other strategies on that market to skip.

## Price / tick / min-size issues observed

- **Real Polymarket tick on sub-$0.10 markets is $0.001, not $0.01.** The scanner currently
  assumes $0.01. This makes `spread_capture` unable to fire on any tight-priced market,
  because `edge = spread − tick = 0.001 − 0.01 = 0`. A future fix should read the real tick
  from `/markets/{condition_id}` (CLOB API) or from market metadata in the Gamma response.
- **No min-order-size issue today.** `orderMinSize` field exists but is unused (after tick
  confusion was fixed). Paper size of $0.25 is well above any plausible min — typical
  Polymarket markets accept orders as small as $1 notional for live; $0.25 is paper-only.
- **Proposed price is the observed `low_price`** for mean_reversion — sanity-check passes:
  we're not proposing to pay above-ask or below-book. For spread_capture (which fired on
  zero markets), proposed price = `best_bid + tick`.

## Why this trade could fail

- Detroit Pistons are currently a ~3.25% probability to win the 2026 NBA Finals. The base
  rate in the NBA Finals market is one winner out of 30 teams, roughly 3.3% if uniform —
  Detroit is priced *at* that uniform baseline. The mean-reversion hypothesis ("rerate
  upward") therefore requires *news* that Detroit is more likely than uniform — a winning
  streak, a return-from-injury, or a playoff advance. **None of those is observed in the
  current information set.**
- 67 days is a long interim window. Basketball playoffs progress weekly; any single loss
  could drift the price down below entry.
- Orderbook depth at $0.0325 is unmeasured. The $342k liquidity stat is market-wide, not
  depth-at-price.
