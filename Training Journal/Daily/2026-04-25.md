## Patterns observed

1. **Premature exit is systemic, not strategy-specific.** 14 of 15 strategies produce
   premature_exit-diagnosed trades. Worst concentration: Coint (62.5% of 8), MoneyMaker
   (59.5% of 232), FVG (56.8% of 37). See [[../Patterns/premature-exit|Patterns/premature-exit]].
2. **The most common trade outcome across the whole library is `fast_loss`** (1,074 trades,
   32.5%) — more than any winner category. The library's natural distribution skews toward
   losing trades that resolve quickly.
3. **`missed_continuation` appears twice in the schema** — as an `outcome` (524 trades, about
   the realised-vs-MFE gap) and as an `exit_diagnosis` (326 trades, attributed to the exit rule
   specifically). They overlap heavily but aren't equal. The outcome label is the broader
   symptom; the diagnosis label is the narrower cause-attribution.
4. **`VWAPMeanReversionStrategy`'s low missed-continuation rate (0.76%) is a trap** — it looks
   like a well-captured strategy but has a 1.8% win rate. Few wins means few opportunities to
   miss continuation on. A concept is only informative when normalised by the population that
   could produce it.

## Mistakes (mine, in how I reasoned today)

1. **Overweighting the Coint result.** Coint trade_0005 (+26.57%) is visually compelling but
   the strategy has n=8 and the "win rate" claim from an 8-trade sample is statistically
   unreliable. I kept reaching for it as the canonical premature-exit illustration. It's a
   representative example; it is not proof.
2. **Nearly created a `slow_loss` concept page** — the 565 trades labelled slow_loss deserve
   one, and the user's task said "do not change structure," which I took as binding. Flagging
   here so I don't forget: `wiki/concepts/slow-loss.md` needs a cross-library expansion on
   the next structural change.
3. **Conflated "early-exit flag" with "premature_exit ≥ 45%."** These have different thresholds
   (10/15 vs 8/15). I kept both numbers honest in the synthesis page but the first-pass
   phrasing suggested they were the same. Note: diagnostic-flag thresholds are not the same
   as rate thresholds.

## Insights

- **Exit redesign has the biggest leverage per unit of engineering effort.** The
  strong_entry_weak_exit archetype has 1 strategy; fully_inefficient has 12. In the
  fully_inefficient bucket, fixing the exit alone won't save the strategy, but it's the
  first thing to try because it's cheaper than retraining an entry rule.
- **Pair concentration is silent risk.** BTC + ETH = 73% of library trades. If those two
  assets correlate tightly (they do), "15 strategies" overstates the diversification. The
  effective strategy count against pair-regime dependence is closer to 3-4.
- **The 336 efficient_exit trades are the training set** — if I want to know what a working
  exit looks like in this data, they're the examples. I didn't study them today. They're
  not in the library sections I built. Add them to the next daily's trades-reviewed list.
